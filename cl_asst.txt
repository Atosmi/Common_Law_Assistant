In common law systems, stare decisis influences judicial decisions in several ways:

1. It ensures consistency and predictability: By following established precedents, judges can ensure that similar cases are decided in a consistent manner, making it easier for parties to anticipate outcomes.
2. It promotes stability and predictability: Stare decisis helps maintain the rule of law by preventing courts from making arbitrary or whimsical decisions.
3. It guides judicial decision-making: Judges rely on previous court decisions to inform their own decisions, taking into account the reasoning and facts presented in those cases.
